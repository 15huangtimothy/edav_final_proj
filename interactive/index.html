Is our planet green?
Climate Change is becoming a bigger concern.
Increasing emissions of CO2 is likely to be causing climate change.
But Total CO2 Emission Rate is actually decreasing.
CO2 Emission Rate =
CO2 Emission/Energy Consumed
Carbon Emissions in the World
The US has contributed the most CO2 (in million tonnes) in 2004, until it was exceeded by China in 2005. CO2 emissions in the US has been consistent throughout, while emissions in China has been growing exponentially.
Total Renewable Energy in the World
Total Renewable Energy = Wind + Solar + Others (Biomass, Biofuels).
Europe, US and China are the biggest contributors.
Hydro Energy Production in the World
China has been focusing on increasing Hydroelectric energy generation. Other countries have not experienced the same growth.
Note: Hydro is not included in Total Renewables, but is a form of renewable energy.
Wind Energy Production in the World
Wind energy is relatively new type of renewable energy that has gained traction since 2010.
Solar Energy Production in the World
Like Wind energy, Solar is also relatively new. China, US and Japan are the top contributors of Solar energy generation.
